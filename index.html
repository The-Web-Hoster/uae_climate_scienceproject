About the UAE Climate
The UAE has a desert climate characterized by extremely hot summers, mild winters, low rainfall, and very high UV levels. Monitoring environmental data such as temperature, UV index, and daylight hours is important for health, sustainability, and climate awareness.


Why This Data Matters
High temperatures and UV levels impact human health, water consumption, and energy use. Live environmental dashboards like this help people make safer daily decisions and raise awareness about climate change and environmental protection.


How to follow UV index properly
The UV Index is a scale from 1 to 11+, and the higher the number, the greater the risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure.
Here’s a quick guide:
  • 0-2 (Low): Safe to be outside. Minimal sun protection needed.
  • 3-5 (Moderate): Some risk. Shade, sunscreen, and sunglasses are smart.
  • High (6-7): High risk; take precautions such as wearing sunscreen and protective clothing.
  • Very High (8-10): Very high risk; avoid sun exposure during midday hours.
  • Extreme (11 and above): Extreme risk; take all precautions to avoid sun exposure.
